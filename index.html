Trombone Trolley

Toot Your Way Through the Wild West

About

Saddle up for a musical journey through the Wild West! Trombone Trolley is an innovative VR experience where you hold a physical trombone prop and match the pitch of classic western tunes to power your desert cart down the railroad tracks. The better you play, the faster you go! Inspired by the rhythm game Trombone Champ, this immersive VR adventure combines hand tracking, physical props, and musical gameplay to create an unforgettable experience. Can you hit the right notes and reach the end of the line before the song runs out?

How to Play

Getting Started

Put on your VR headset and grab the physical trombone prop. The game uses advanced hand tracking to detect your grip and movements, making the virtual trombone feel like a real instrument. Once you're holding the trombone, you'll find yourself on a cart in the middle of a vast desert landscape, ready to begin your musical journey.

Playing Your Tune

Listen carefully to the western melody playing and move the trombone slide to the correct position, then blow into the physical trombone to play the note! The audio detection system evaluates your timing and performance. Visual indicators with beautiful rim-lit shaders help guide you to the correct slide position. The more accurately you hit the notes at the right time, the faster your cart accelerates down the tracks. Perfect timing will have you zooming through the canyon!

Reaching Your Destination

Your goal is to travel as far down the railroad tracks as possible before the song ends. Each note you hit perfectly adds momentum to your cart, while missed notes slow you down. Watch the stunning desert scenery blur past as you master the melody. How far can you make it before the final note?

Technology

Hand Tracking & Physical Trombone Integration

One of the biggest innovations was creating a rich physical trombone interaction system. We customized Unity's built-in hand tracking to accurately detect hand position and grip on the physical prop, mapping it to the virtual trombone in real-time. Players don't just move the slide—they actually blow into the trombone to produce sound, which our audio detection system analyzes for pitch accuracy. This dual-input system (sliding + blowing) creates an authentic trombone playing experience that feels natural and immersive.

Audio Detection & Beat Mapping

The core gameplay mechanic relies on audio detection to evaluate player performance. When players blow into the trombone at the right moment with the slide in the correct position, the audio system detects their input and evaluates it against the beat map. The beat map system synchronizes the musical challenges with the cart's movement along the tracks, creating a rhythm-based progression system. This tight integration between audio input, slide positioning, visual feedback, and gameplay rewards allows players to feel the satisfaction of hitting the right notes and adjusting their technique on the fly.

Advanced Shading & Procedural Generation

A major innovation in our project was the custom shader work, particularly the note visualization system with beautiful rim lighting effects that guide players to the correct pitch positions. The shaders create an eye-catching visual language that makes the musical gameplay intuitive and satisfying. Additionally, we implemented procedural generation for the desert environment, creating varied and expansive landscapes that generate dynamically as the cart travels down the rails, ensuring each journey feels fresh while maintaining optimal performance.

Development Journey

Challenges & Solutions

Creating a convincing physical trombone interaction proved to be one of our most significant challenges. We needed to combine sliding motion tracking with breath detection for an authentic playing experience. VR hand tracking systems are optimized for bare hands, so integrating a physical prop required careful customization of Unity's hand tracking to work reliably with our trombone design.

Our Solution: We customized Unity's built-in hand tracking system specifically for our use case, creating a robust interaction model that accurately captures both the slide position and the player's blowing action. The audio detection system recognizes when players blow at the right moments, while our custom shaders provide clear visual feedback through rim-lit note indicators. This combination creates a rich, responsive instrument that feels authentic in the player's hands.

Another challenge was maintaining visual clarity and performance while the procedurally generated desert environment streamed in around the moving cart. Our shader optimizations and smart generation systems ensured smooth performance even with the complex rim lighting effects on the musical notes.

Inspiration & Related Work

Trombone Trolley draws heavy inspiration from Trombone Champ, the viral rhythm game that brought trombone gameplay to mainstream audiences with its hilarious and challenging mechanics. We loved the core concept but wanted to explore how it could translate to VR with physical props and immersive environments. Games like Beat Saber and Pistol Whip demonstrated how rhythm-based VR experiences can be incredibly engaging, while titles like Red Dead Redemption influenced our visual direction for the wild western aesthetic.

We also looked at real-world examples of musical instruments in VR, such as SoundStage VR and various virtual music creation tools. These experiences showed us the potential for expressive musical interaction in virtual space, but we wanted to focus on accessibility and immediate fun rather than simulation complexity.

Lessons Learned

Physical Props + Breath Input = Maximum Immersion: The combination of holding a physical trombone and actually blowing into it significantly increased player immersion. The dual-input system made the experience feel remarkably authentic compared to controller-only prototypes.

Visual Feedback Through Shaders Is Crucial: Our custom rim-lit shaders for the note indicators weren't just aesthetically pleasing—they became essential for gameplay clarity. The visual language helped players understand pitch targets instantly.

Audio Detection Needs Careful Tuning: Detecting breath-based audio input required extensive calibration to work reliably across different players and blowing intensities. The beat map system needed to be forgiving enough for newcomers while still rewarding accurate timing.

Procedural Generation Enables Scalability: Generating the desert environment procedurally allowed us to create seemingly endless tracks without manual content creation, while maintaining visual variety and performance.

Unity's Hand Tracking is Customizable: While Unity's built-in hand tracking worked well out of the box, customizing it for our specific trombone interaction unlocked much more reliable and natural-feeling controls.

Playtesting Revealed Unexpected Joy: Even players who struggled with pitch matching enjoyed the experience, often laughing at their attempts and trying to improve. The game's forgiving nature made it accessible to non-musicians while still rewarding skilled play.
